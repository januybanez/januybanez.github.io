Metro Manila (CNN Philippines, May 8)

Filipina actress Ellen Adarna may face legal action after publicly accusing a minor of being a "paparazzi" in a viral video.


A Facebook post from Eleila Santos' mother, Myra, said Monday the family has begun seeking legal assistance
regarding the incident and demanded a public apology from the celebrity-actress.


"My lawyer indicates that you will have five days to reply to this letter. Should you fail or refuse to do so,
we will not hesitate to take the appropriate legal actions necessary under the circumstances, to protect and
uphold the best interest and welfare of our 17 year old, Eleila A. Santos," Myra's statement said.


Myra mentioned that her family members have been reaching out to Adarna privately to resolve the issue but
instead were shunned by the actress. She said she expected her to act professionally given Adarna's status as
a celebrity.
"We initially took it lightly, thinking things would be resolved quickly and the 30-year old you would give the
two minors the benefit of the doubt. Even her aunt wrote a private message to you, and mentioned she is a minor.
However, we did not hear anything from you," Myra said.


After the lack of response, Myra resorted to seeing the video as an attempt to "defame" her daughter.


"She is my only living child. She is just 17 years old…As much as no one has the right to breach your privacy,
you have no right to ruin my daughter's reputation," the statement said.


Adarna posted her Instagram video explicitly showing Eleila in a Makati restaurant on May 4 with the caption
"When you PAP us, we PAP you. It's a tie. #PAParazziMoves."


Eleila denied the accusation and shared her own video of the incident, insisting she was just focusing the camera
on her bowl of ramen.


Ellen Adarna gained popularity for playing antagonist roles in soap operas. She is currently on hiatus, and is linked
to John Lloyd Cruz, another actor whose career is on hold.
