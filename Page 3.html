Scene stealers: 5 candidates who stole the limelight in Miss Universe 2018

Metro Manila (CNN Philippines, December 17)

Both beauty pageant fanatics and ordinary spectators were treated to an incredible show Monday morning
as this year's Miss Universe competition displayed the right amount of thrill and excitement.


From hard-hitting questions to candidates that made history, the annual prestigious beauty contest did
not disappoint.


Philippine bet Catriona Gray emerged as the winner after the three-hour coronation night held in Bangkok,
Thailand. She bested 93 other candidates to bring the Mikimoto crown back to the country.


Gray, however, wasn't the only one to steal the show during the pageant proper.


Here are some of the candidates who stole the limelight in the Miss Universe 2018


Miss Costa Rica gives Steve Harvey a valuable piece of advice
Miss Costa Rica Natalia Carvajal captured netizens' attention when she jokingly dished out a valuable
piece of advice for the competition host Steve Harvey.


During the top 20 interviews, Harvey asked Carvajal-- who is also a television host in her country-- for
some advice in their chosen profession. The candidate playfully adhered.


"I think you're doing good, the outfit is fine. The smile is always great. But I have to give you one advice
just for the future, just in case. Come closer because I don't want anyone to hear," Carvajal poked fun at the mainstay host.


"If they give you like a really, really important envelope, try to read carefully, okay?"


Ya'll just won't let it go, huh?" Harvey then jokingly addressed the audience.


Harvey most notably broke the internet in 2015 when he announced the wrong winner during that year's Miss Universe
competition. Miss Colombia was already doing her winner's walk when Harvey reentered the stage and announced that it
was Philippines' Pia Wurtzbach who actually won the competition.


Miss Jamaica's jitters?
Nerves seemed to get in the way of Miss Jamaica Emily Maddison during her opening statement in the competition.


The 19-year-old candidate paused several times during her speech.


"I started my Miss Universe journey, despite my insecurities. I know that… I know that I actually started to work on my
insecurities," she said, stumbling over the words.


But with the encouragement of the crowd as well as her fellow candidates, Maddison was able to get through the moment.


"And now that I've realized that a queen defines her own perfection. I know I have what it takes to inspire, to relate, and
most importantly, to comfort young women across the world and across the universe," she closed.


Miss Vietnam wins hearts of netizens
After getting involved in a controversy with fellow contestants, Miss Vietnam H'Hen Nie stole the limelight during coronation night.


Netizens complimented the candidate for her sheer confidence as well as for "breaking the society's standards" with her look.


"I am an ethnic minority. I'm (expected) to get married at 14 but no. I (got) education from nothing, and here I am. (If) I can do (it),
you can do it," she said in her opening statement.


Before the pageant, Miss Vietnam was thrust into the spotlight after Miss USA Sarah Rose Summers-in a viral clip-- allegedly mocked non-English
speaking Miss Universe contestants.


Catriona Gray's Mayon-inspired evening gown
It was her signature slow-mo turn during the preliminary swimsuit competition that captured the hearts of spectators.


But on coronation night, Gray stole the show during the evening gown portion.


Miss Universe 2018 Catriona Gray blazed the stage with her fiery Mayon volcano-inspired evening gown. By wearing the Mak Tumang creation, Gray paid
homage to her hometown in Albay.


In the press conference following her victory, Gray explained she chose a red gown after her mother-- years ago-- dreamed she won Miss Universe wearing
a red dress.


During the Top 5 portion, Gray's red ensemble literally stood out in a sea of silver.


Miss Spain makes history
Miss Spain Angela Ponce made history as the first ever transwoman to walk the stage in the history of the pageant.


"I have been limited to not being able to do as many things as I would have liked to," she said in Spanish during the pageant's special feature. "So I
developed the ability to create, to dream, that someday I could make it a reality. I feel like I have done that being here today."


Ponce failed to crack the Top 15, but she won the hearts not only of her community, but also of the viewers from around the world.


During a special final walk, Ponce strutted in the pageant runway, removed her sash, and proudly raised it high like a flag. The gesture earned her a
standing ovation from the crowd in attendance.


"I don't need to win Miss Universe. I just need to be here," she said after the competition.
